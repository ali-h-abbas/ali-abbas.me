*The following is a summary & reflections relating to the workshop discussion hosted on September 7th, 2025 in collaboration with [Electric Sunshine Co.](https://www.electricsunshine.co/)*

---
## An Origin in Grief

This was my debut as a public [[10-Words/storyteller]], an event where I intentionally explored and experienced the powerful effects of collective ritual. It was inspired by a number of key influences percolating in my life at this time. My family very recently experienced an unexpected loss in the passing of my aunt, and I had channeled my sorrow into reading psychotherapist Francis Weller's [*The Wild Edge of Sorrow*](https://amzn.to/47DS2WO).

Grief became a gift. It connected me to a deep current of emotion in my heart and made me aware of the collective sorrow all humans carry. We live our lives from day-to-day perhaps forgetting that *everything we love, we will lose.*

As I wrestled with this new emotional reality, my usual whirlwind of productivity slowed to a steady, conscious movement forward. For months, I had been grappling with a central question on my entrepreneurial journey: **What does it mean to present myself authentically?** At the root of this was a fear - a fear of *truly* stepping into what it means to be me. The risk I feared was that with a more authentic projection of self, there was more weight if I was to be rejected.

The experience of this loss however crushed this fear into a soluble dust. Was I going to face my own death with the regret of a life unlived?

From the container of my grief, I found the purpose to offer something to my community—an event that would connect us back to what it means to be human.

## The Seed of New Beginnings

With that guiding question in mind, the time had come to turn my personal practice into a public offering. The event I envisioned would be a space for collective exploration, a true new beginning where we could all experience the lunar cycles together.

I wanted the experience to be simple, accessible, and deeply human, marrying the best practices of group facilitation with the intuitive capacity humans have for ritual. We gathered in a beautiful space, surrounded by personal symbols and objects that grounded us in a shared intention. We rooted ourselves in the present moment, understanding that **everything we do is a ritual**—from how we wake up to how we celebrate birthdays. Rituals provide context, and context allows the mind to move beyond logistical problems and root into the broader realities of being.

I opened the evening by sharing my perspective on the importance of storytelling and how the lunar cycle serves as a model for our universal experiences of growth, decay, and rebirth. I wanted to remind everyone that **language is rooted in experience**, and that our ancestors developed all language through the medium of stories. The goal was never a dry lecture, but an experiential marriage of pedagogy and mysticism, designed to fill the cups of attendees and support them on their individual journeys.

Ultimately, I wanted people to walk away with a practical understanding of the symbolic grammar demonstrated by the cycles of the cosmos. This practice has influenced great kings, queens, mystics, and heroes throughout history, prompting them to take actions that shaped our world. For me, a deep connection to these cycles has breathed joy and love into my own work and relationships. This was the bridge I wanted to build—the age-old practice of storytelling and singing around a communal fire.

As a coach and guide for individuals and entrepreneurs, I intended to use group facilitation to enable conversations that tied us together to our collective "prompts." The inspiration, of course, was the moon and its cycles. This is where the partnership with Cynthia from Electric Sunshine was so potent; her experience in creating events like this helped to bring my vision to life.

To truly understand this lesson, the experience had to be hands-on. We set up the room with circular seating and invited participants to contribute to our altar in the middle. This simple act of sharing what we had brought to the space created an effective **container of togetherness**, which allowed my initial sharing to serve as a conduit, enabling a collective conversation where everyone could explore what the themes of the current time meant to them, from their own unique life perspectives.
## The Eclipse in Pisces

There's a reason we chose this particular moment for our gathering. The lunar eclipse marks a point of dramatic emphasis in the ongoing dance of the sun and moon. If you picture the movement of the cosmos from our perspective on Earth as a great clock, some of the hands move slowly, and some move quickly. The drama of our lives unfolds in the moments when these hands come together: in harmony, tension, or, in the case of an eclipse, a powerful convergence.

Eclipses are a particularly dramatic cycle because they involve the two most prominent lights in our sky: the sun and the moon. The hands of the sun and moon travel through a 29-day cycle of conjunction, opposition, and back again, marked by the new and full moons. A lunar eclipse is a special emphasis, a punctuation mark in this cycle, happening only when the sun and moon's alignment occurs precisely at the point where their orbital paths intersect.

![[attachments/When-is-an-Eclipse-Possible-TC.jpg|When an Eclipse is possible|700]]

We were gathering not just under a full moon, but under an eclipse. This was not a moment for dry, academic explanation; It was a time to feel the palpable shift in the quality of time and sense the rhythm and polarity of our lives. This event was my hypothesis in action: What happens when we explore these subtle shifts together? In joining in ritual we were participating actively in cycles, experiencing in community the weight and significance of a moment when the lights in our sky paused and transformed.
## The Quality of Time

It was a real and verifiable *channeling* of the quality *of* time. A collective act of undivided attention towards the core subject of our individual experience.

This quality *of* time is a phenomena commonly spoken to in the creative arts where the observation of the entirety of a thing - such as a harmony in music. The individual notes are just components; without the context of the surrounding parts, they are meaningless. This is what we did together.

As stories flowed through the room, evolving with each participant's reflections and personal anecdotes, the textures and tonalities of our lives formed a collective harmony. It was a joy to see people light up as they were seen and heard, as they got things off their chests and shared their intentions with present eyes and ears.

As we closed our container with a sound healing, the space reverberated with the release of what we sought to let go of and the welcoming of what we were hoping and dreaming to bring forward.

Leaving the container, I observed new connections forming among participants. I enjoyed engaging deeply with those around me, hearing what they were working through and what tools they were bringing with them. People shared strategies, personal practices, and creative outlets, offering each other a new kind of support.

We all left the room with our cups filled, carrying the shared rhythm of the evening with us. It was a true testament to the power of collective ritual, and I look forward to the opportunity to do it again.
