Fire is the passion to be.
According to the philosophers of ancient times, fire was the most vital element, and also the most rare.

As the least dense of the 4 classical elements, it was observed that fire reaches upwards.
It scatters.
In its scattering it leaves both destruction and renewal.

To fire it was attributed all things dynamic and changing.
Fire in its creative was pronounced loudly to the world when Moses met the burning bush.
“I CREATE THAT WHICH WILL BE”
“I AM WHAT I AM”

Brought together under the flame of the current moment.
We spoke to what it meant to tend our fires.
Of when we know fire is speaking.
Where we felt it.

What it was like to ignite our passion.
To be fired up.
To have sparks fly in conversation.
To be blazed with fervour.

We explored what it meant to harness that with safety.
A martial artist uses fire, but dances with it with respect for its potential for violence.

How do we speak our words of truth.
Knowing those words may harm others?
When is the fires rage a welcome clearing of the overgrowth, 
And when is it a great conflagration?

We spoke of what it meant to train our vessels to hold fire.
Of the power of experimentation and exposure therapy.
Of speaking the truth in manageable proportions.
Of the power of Time.

That true control of our circumstances,
Comes from trusting all is already under control.
