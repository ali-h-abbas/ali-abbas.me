The central cosmological framework of [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/Neoplatonism]], describing how all reality flows from [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/The One]] through successive levels of being. This process unfolds not through creation in time but as an eternal, necessary overflow - like light radiating from the sun or heat from fire. Themetaphysical architecture moves from absolute unity through [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/Nous]] (divine intellect), then [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/Psyche]] (soul), finally reaching the material [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/Kosmos]] where multiplicity and separation reach their furthest extent.

The descent of [[soul]] into matter represents both a cosmic necessity and an individual predicament. As souls move away from their source, they experience increasing [[fragmentation]] - torn between their higher rational nature that yearns for unity and their lower aspects entangled in material existence. This is not a fall in the moral sense but rather the natural consequence of emanation reaching its outermost limits, where the light of the One grows dim and souls forget their divine origin. The doctrine of [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/anamnesis]] names this forgetting and the possibility of its reversal - [[philosophy]] and [[contemplation]] as the practices by which the soul remembers what it truly is.

The framework establishes reality as a great chain of being, where each level derives its existence from what precedes it while simultaneously turning back toward its source. This double movement - *proodos* (procession outward) and *epistrophe* (return inward) - defines the fundamental rhythm of existence. [[Henosis]] names the culmination of return: the soul's reunification with the One from which it emanated. The concept profoundly influenced [[Islamic philosophy]], [[Christian mysticism]], and [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Kabbalah and Judeo-Christian Mysteries/Kabbalah]], providing a sophisticated model for understanding how transcendent unity relates to manifest multiplicity without compromising divine perfection.

**Key characteristics of emanation:**
- Occurs through necessity, not will or choice
- Each level contains less unity and more multiplicity than what precedes it
- The process is both [[ontological]] (describing being) and [[epistemological]] (describing knowing)
- Material reality represents the furthest point from [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/The One]], where emanation exhausts itself
