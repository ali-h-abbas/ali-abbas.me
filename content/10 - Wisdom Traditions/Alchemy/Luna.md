# Luna

The Moon as alchemical and psychological symbol, Luna represents the feminine principle within the [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Alchemy/The Great Work\|opus]] - receptive, reflective, cyclical, and intimately connected to the [[20 - The Human System/23 - Inner Architecture/Jung/unconscious]]. Paired with [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Alchemy/Sol]] (the Sun, masculine, active, conscious), Luna participates in the [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Alchemy/coniunctio]], the sacred marriage whose fruit is the [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Alchemy/Philosopher's Stone]] or, in psychological terms, [[wholeness]].

In [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Alchemy/alchemy]], Luna corresponds to silver, to water, to the volatile and mutable aspects of matter and [[10 - Wisdom Traditions/Western/Neoplatonism/Psyche]]. Where Sol burns steadily, Luna waxes and wanes; where Sol illuminates directly, Luna offers reflected light - knowledge that comes obliquely, through [[dreams]], [[intuition]], and [[symbol]] rather than rational analysis. The alchemists depicted Luna as queen, bride, sister, or mother to Sol, emphasizing that the Great Work requires both principles in dynamic relationship. Neglect of Luna produces a consciousness that is bright but brittle, all clarity and no depth.

[[20 - The Human System/23 - Inner Architecture/Jung/Carl Jung]] associated Luna with the [[anima]] in men - the feminine soul-image that mediates between [[20 - The Human System/23 - Inner Architecture/Jung/ego]] and [[20 - The Human System/23 - Inner Architecture/Jung/unconscious]] - though the symbol exceeds any single correspondence. Luna governs the realm of [[feeling]], of imaginal perception, of the body's wisdom and its tidal rhythms. Modern culture's privileging of solar values (productivity, rationality, constant illumination) may represent a collective Luna-deficit, manifesting as alienation from [[interiority]], nature, and the feminine. The moon reminds us that darkness has its own intelligence, that what cannot be seen directly may still be known.
