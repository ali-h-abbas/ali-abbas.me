Agency represents the fundamental capacity to act upon one's intentions and shape reality according to internal drives rather than external forces. At its core, agency describes the intersection between [[20 - The Human System/30-neurophysiology/Consciousness]] and [[causality]] - the point where awareness transforms into directed action that creates [[30 -  Systems and Dynamics/change]] in the world.

The concept extends beyond simple free-[[000 - Core Ideas/Will]] or choice-making. True agency emerges from alignment between one's deepest motivations and the capacity to pursue them, requiring both internal clarity about what genuinely calls to us and the practical power to respond. This distinguishes agency from mere reaction or compliance; it involves actively authoring one's path rather than passively following predetermined scripts. The phenomenon connects intimately with [[authenticity]], as genuine agency flows from actions rooted in one's essential nature rather than adopted personas or social conditioning.

The cultivation of agency often requires confronting [[shadow]] work and examining where we've unconsciously surrendered our power to external authorities or internalized limitations.
