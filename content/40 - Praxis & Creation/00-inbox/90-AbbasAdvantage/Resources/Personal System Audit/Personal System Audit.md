If you're tired of playing a character in someone else's script—or if you feel blocked from stepping into your full creative power—you have to audit the **sub-optimal patterns** running in your inner world.

The core of this blockage is often a subconscious [[50 - Transmissions/10-Words/System Audit for the Self\|Pattern]]: a cluster of emotionally charged ideas that operate outside your conscious control, sabotaging your best intentions. I've based this worksheet off of [Steven Kessler's 5 Personality Patterns.](https://amzn.to/4799sIY), which you can read some of my reflections on [[50 - Transmissions/10-Words/System Audit for the Self\|here]].

Otherwise, dive in and use this 3-step audit to diagnose your most active Pattern and begin your personal **Reboot.**
*Note: If you're in active crisis, and your emotions are too big to hold, please be kind to yourself, seek the required help, and come back to this only when you have the capacity.*
## Step 1: Map the Trigger
### Focus: The Response (The Pattern you need to break)
Before you can resolve the internal conflict, you have to map the pattern in your observable reality. Choose one specific, recent moment where you stopped yourself from creating, initiating, collaborating, or moving forward.

| Personality Pattern   | Question to Locate the Pattern (The Observable Blockage)                                                                                                   | Example Responses to Actions & Observable Patterns                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Leaving (L)**    | Where did your awareness go? Did you retreat physically, mentally, or energetically when intensity arose?                                                  | **Withdrawal/Dissociation:** I physically left the room or energetically “spaced out” when the conversation became emotionally charged. I neglected basic physical needs like sleep or food. I began talking in abstractions to avoid personal feelings.                                |
| **2. Merging (M)**    | How did you attempt to secure connection or avoid abandonment? Did you collapse, over-accommodate, or pull on others for validation?                       | **Seeking Rescue/Over-Giving:** I abandoned my own goal to focus entirely on someone else’s needs or pleasure. I collapsed in frustration or helplessness when faced with a difficulty I felt I couldn't handle alone. I couldn't say “no” to a request.                                |
| **3. Enduring (E)**   | How did you refuse to move or express yourself? Did you procrastinate, become heavy/stuck, or secretly resist an external demand?                          | **Resistance/Sabotage:** I delayed taking action until the opportunity passed. I agreed outwardly but unconsciously sabotaged the plan through passive-aggressive inaction or "accidents". I hunkered down and refused to communicate or reveal what I truly wanted.                    |
| **4. Aggressive (A)** | Where did you attempt to assert dominance or control? Did you override your own needs or the boundaries of others?                                         | **Domination/Force:** I pushed myself or others past our limits with the attitude of "I can handle it". I blasted someone with anger or criticism to force a desired outcome. I ignored my body’s signals of weakness or fear.                                                          |
| **5. Rigid (R)**      | What standard did you try to maintain, and what spontaneous feeling did you repress? Did you focus on the perfect process, or correct the flaws of others? | **Perfection/Correction:** I became paralyzed by analysis, needing to map every step before beginning (analysis paralysis). I intensely criticized myself or others for minor mistakes or imperfections. I suppressed spontaneous desires or strong emotions to maintain control/order. |

> [!NOTE] Reflect
> Which of the patterns best describe what happened? Was there more than one? If there was, how were they competing with one another?

## Step 2: Locate the Conflict (The Complex)

### Focus: The Thought/Feeling 
Now, move deeper. Immediately before or during the moment of avoidance (your trigger), what was the exact thought or feeling that dominated your consciousness? This feeling or thought is the voice of the Pattern.

| Personality Pattern   | Core Thought/Voice (The Patterned Narrative)                                                                   | Core Somatic/Emotional Experience (The Core Neurosis)                                                                 |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Leaving (L)**    | "Gotta get away!". "I am not safe here". "No one cares".                                                       | **Fear**, a feeling of being shattered or fragmented, generalized anxiety and hyper-vigilance.                        |
| **2. Merging (M)**    | "I can’t do it," or "You have to do it for me". "I am not enough". "If I disappoint you, I will be abandoned". | **Shame and Self-Doubt**, a debilitating sense of deficiency or emptiness in the core, leading to emotional collapse. |
| **3. Enduring (E)**   | "You can’t make me. Leave me alone". "I can’t get it, or I’ve failed". "I don’t deserve to take up space".     | **Resentment, Guilt, and Shame**. Feeling heavy, stuck, or numb inside, constantly burdened.                          |
| **4. Aggressive (A)** | "I have to do it all myself". "I can and I will". "I knew I couldn’t trust you".                               | **Anger**, underlying **frozen terror**. Feeling a tense alertness, readiness to fight, or guardedness.               |
| **5. Rigid (R)**      | "I should/I shouldn’t". "What is appropriate?". "I have to work harder, only perfection is lovable".           | **Anger, Judgment, and Resentment**. Anxiety triggered by disorder, feeling constricted, hollow, or stiff inside.     |


> [!NOTE] Reflect
> Can you associate a thought to a feeling in a body? Numbness can be a feeling as well. How capable are you at noticing what your nervous system is signalling?

## Step 3: Define the Code

### Focus: The Statement of Agency

The Pattern's power is in its unconscious loop. You break the pattern by consciously defining a new Code—a statement that reclaims your creative agency and reframes your state. Your Code should directly counter the Diagnosis you found in Step 2. Some examples are provided below.

| Personality Pattern | Statement of Agency (The New Operating Principle)                                    | Key Strategy to Unwind the Pattern (The Rewire)                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1. Leaving (L)      | I claim my body; I am safe and embodied here and now.                                | Grounding and Centering: Shift attention from fear in the mind back to body sensations and connection to the Earth (Ground/Core). Choose to stay and live.                                       |
| 2. Merging (M)      | I am intrinsically worthy and full; I reference my core and act on my own behalf.    | Self-Referencing and Boundaries: Stand on your own two feet, connect to your core, and allow the earth's support to fill you. Practice self-care and self-care is enough.                        |
| 3. Enduring (E)     | I move forward; my self-expression is safe, valid, and worthy of space.              | Mobilization and Expansion: Actively move the body to release stuck energy (e.g., Doubt Shout, movement). Claim your personal space and direct energy outward toward goals.                      |
| 4. Aggressive (A)   | I surrender to support; I choose connection and contained power over forced control. | Connecting and Containing: Connect to a loving, larger force/Divine to feel held and supported. Practice _down-regulating_ the system and functioning from a calm, centered inner state.         |
| 5. Rigid (R)        | I trust my inner wisdom; my value is based on my being, not my performance.          | Softening and Feeling: Shift attention from correctness to pleasure, joy, or softness. Learn to sense body sensations and feelings as valid guidance, rather than relying only on external rules |
> [!NOTE] Action Plan
> How might you anchor yourself into this new found agency in future scenarios? Who might be able to help you?
