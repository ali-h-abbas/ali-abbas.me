Ali went to high school with a dumb-flip phone, experienced the peak of the blackberry by the 12th grade, while some of the cool kids brandished these new fandangled "iPhones."

With good grades Ali's professional options were open to him, but the thought of the extended schooling involved of snapping into a profession like Doctor or Engineer like his parents might have wanted, made him churn internally.

Ali opted to follow the business stream as he entered the 11th grade. The classes were easy enough that he was able to finish his math and english homework during them in the afternoon, gifting himself an abundance of free-time to go home and tinker with his various electronic-computer toys.

Ali enrolled in the University of Toronto Scarborough's Business Management program. Pragmatically, it was a 10 minute drive from home, and offered a co-op program that promised a good chance of job prospects with the Big 4 accounting firms.

Ali quickly realized he hated accounting, and accounting hated him. By 2nd year he switched specialist programs into Strategic Management - which he valued due to the flexibility to take elective courses spanning the humanities.

On his first co-op term, Ali worked as a client-service representative at AGF Trust. Here Ali discovered he has a pretty good telephone voice if he put enough effort into it. During this period of time Ali spent a lot of time talking into headsets, as he showed up dutifully to his gaming rig each night for online gaming with his friends.

His second co-op term was at MTS Allstream working in competitive intelligence. Ali had no experience doing this but the fact he knew a bunch of computery-words helped him out in the interview. He was given various research projects relating to mapping how the trending tech at the time could help MTS's sales team in selling business internet. That was about as fun as it sounds.

Taking all of the MTS lingo he had learned (Big Data! IOT!), Ali hit the jackpot at a networking event on campus where he was able to form the popular buzzwords of the time into a coherent enough story to impress the hiring team at Microsoft Canada.

Ali's first role of his 9 year term at Microsoft was as an intern at the Sales Operations desk, where Ali supported forecasting rhythms with reporting, analysis, and hounding down sales reps for CRM updates.

Graduating school and finishing his internship, Ali landed at Microsoft on a contract with Adecco, now supporting Sales Operations for the Professional Services Division.
